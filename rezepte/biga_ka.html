KitchenAid: Neapolitan Pizza with 100% Biga

Makes 6 pizzas (280g each), 48h cold fermentation

I know it's tough to knead 100% biga doughs with the KitchenAid, but so far this has always worked very well!


Biga:
  • 1000g pizza flour (Tipo 00, e.g. Caputo Cuoco)
  • 500ml lukewarm water (important for easier kneading)
  • 2g fresh yeast

Dissolve the yeast in the lukewarm water. Add to the flour in a bowl (or pizza box), shake gently until the water is absorbed, and scrape down the sides. Rest for 30 minutes at room temperature, then refrigerate for 20–24 hours.


Final Dough:
  • 200ml ice-cold water
  • 30g salt

Remove the Biga from the fridge and cut it into very small pieces. In the KitchenAid, add a splash of water and knead on speed 1 for 5 minutes, adding all the water in small splashes about every minute. Add the salt with the last splash. After 5 minutes, cover the dough in the bowl with a towel and let it rest for 5–10 minutes to help the Biga dissolve better.
Continue kneading at higher speeds (about 6–7 minutes total, starting at speed 4 and then speed 6) until the dough is smooth.

With crushed ice I achieve a dough temperature below 19°C. Everything sub 25°C should be fine.

Place the dough on an oiled surface, gently stretch and fold it, then form a ball and place it in an oiled bowl. Refrigerate for another 20–24 hours.


Final Steps:

On baking day, divide the dough into 280g balls and gently round them. Let rest at room temperature for 4–6 hours in a pizza box. Ready to bake!
